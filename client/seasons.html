Understanding Seasons in Gardening

Monthly Tips:

.

Gardening changes throughout the year because plants respond to temperature, daylight, and soil conditions. Each season creates different growing opportunities and challenges. Rather than growing everything at once, successful gardening depends on working with the season.

Spring

Spring is a transition season where soil begins to warm and plants restart active growth. It is typically used for establishing early crops and preparing the garden for summer.

  • Best for cool-season and early growth crops.
  • Soil moisture is naturally higher.
  • Risk of late frost may still exist in early spring.

Summer

Spring is a transition season where soil begins to warm and plants restart active growth. It is typically used for establishing early crops and preparing the garden for summer.

  • Best for fruiting and heat-tolerant crops.
  • Higher water demand.
  • Increased pest and heat stress management.

Fall

Fall is a cooling season that supports stable growth and improved flavor development in many crops.

  • Best for leafy greens and root vegetables.
  • Reduced pest pressure.
  • Shorter daylight slows growth gradually.
