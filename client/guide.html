How to Start Gardening

Starting a garden doesn't need to be complicated. You don't need special skills or a large yard—just a few basic steps and some consistency. This guide will walk you through exactly how to get started.

Step 1: Decide What to Grow

Start simple. Choose easy, fast-growing plants so you can see results quickly. Here are some beginner friendly options:

  • Lettuce
  • Basil
  • Tomatoes
  • Strawberries

Step 2: Find the Right Spot

Most plants need sunlight to grow well.

  • Full sun: 6-8 hours of light (best for vegetables)
  • Partial sun: 3-6 hours (good for herbs and leafy greens)
  • Shade: limited options, but some plants still grow

Step 3: Choose Your Setup

You don't need a big yard to start gardening. You can use containers, raised beds, or in-ground gardens.

Step 4: Prepare Your Soil

Healthy soil = Healthy plants

  • Use good-quality potting soil for containers
  • Mix in compost if available
  • Make sure water drains easily

Step 5: Plant and Water Correctly

Follow seed or plant instructions for spacing and depth. Some basic rules include water after planting, keep soil lightly moist (not soaked), water more in hot weather and less in cool weather.

Step 6: Watch, Adjust, and Learn

Gardening is a learning process.

Watch out for:

  • Yellow leaves (often too much water)
  • Slow growth (not enought light)
  • Bugs or holes in leaves
