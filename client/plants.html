Why does the full moon matter for gardening?

Some gardeners follow lunar gardening, a traditional method that times planting with the moon's phases. The idea is that the moon's gravitational pull can influence soil moisture, while increased moonlight may support leaf growth. Because of this, the period around a full moon is often linked to strong above-ground development.

Lunar gardening has been practiced for centuries and is based on the idea that the moon affects water movement in soil, similar to ocean tides. During the waxing phases (from new moon to full moon), gardeners often focus on planting and growth. During the waning phases (after the full moon), attention shifts to pruning, harvesting, and soil care.

What to do

If you'd like to garde by the moon, this is a great time to focus on plants that grow above the soil.

  • Plant leafy greens (lettuce, spinach, kale)
  • Sow herbs and flowering plants
  • Water regularly to help plants grow strong

Moon Phase Guide

  • New Moon --> Plant roots crops (carrots, potatoes, onions)
  • First Quarter --> Plant above-ground crops (beans, tomatoes)
  • Full Moon --> Focus on leafy growth and transplanting
  • Last Quarter --> Prune, weed, and improve soil
